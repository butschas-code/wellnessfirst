Some conversations about “lines” in the land carry folklore; others carry the lived report of a body that will not rest in a certain corner of a home. The honest work is in the **overlap**: we do not need to turn geology into a personality test, and we do not need to dismiss the possibility that a bedroom’s conditions matter.

Geopathic interest, in our usage, is a *question of fit* between a body seeking recovery and a site that can either support that search or fight it. It belongs beside light hygiene, material choices, and sound—not in place of medicine or structural engineering, but in proportion with them.

> Fear makes bad decisions look decisive. Dignity keeps inquiry slow enough to be fair.

## What to hold lightly, what to test

Hold lightly: certainty sold from the outside. Test: repetition—does a change in placement, wiring routing, or shielding correspond to a calmer night over weeks, with other variables held as steady as a life allows? The journal is not a courtroom; it is a lab notebook for adults who are tired of being gaslit and tired of being frightened.

We will return to the practical room audit in our home series, with the same integrative line: **measure, adjust, and keep respect for the unknown**.
